Shanna Swan on Joe Rogan: How Microplastics Are Destroying Human Fertility

Published: April 2026 | Reading time: 9 min

Dr. Shanna Swan — one of the world's leading environmental and reproductive epidemiologists — appeared on the Joe Rogan Experience for a deep-dive into how microplastics and chemical plasticizers are quietly destroying human fertility. Here are the key takeaways everyone needs to know.

The 1% Problem: Fertility Is Declining Everywhere

Dr. Swan's research shows human fertility is declining at approximately 1% per year. Sperm counts have dropped over 50% in the last 50 years. But here's the striking part — it's not just humans.

Parallel Decline: Humans AND Animals

The rate of decline in human fertility mirrors the rate of decline in wildlife populations — both at about 1% per year. Animals aren't choosing to delay childbearing or have busy careers. They're being exposed to the same chemicals through contaminated water, soil, and food supply.

Microplastics: The "Double Damage"

Dr. Swan explains that microplastics and plasticizers (the chemical additives in plastic) aren't the same thing — but they work together to cause maximum harm:

  • Chemical damage: Microplastics carry plasticizers (phthalates, BPA, BPS) into your body — these chemicals disrupt hormones
  • Physical damage: The plastic particles themselves enter cells, causing inflammation similar to asbestos and silica dust

"Microplastics carry the plasticizers along with them. So they do double damage because they carry the chemical harms and they also physically enter the cells."

— Dr. Shanna Swan

The Chef Who Fixed His Testosterone by Ditching Plastic

Joe Rogan shared a story about his friend, Michelin-star chef Philip Franklin Lee, who was experiencing extreme fatigue and had his hormones tested — extremely low testosterone. He then had his microplastic levels tested:

  • Microplastic levels were "off the charts"
  • He eliminated all plastic from his life — stopped drinking from plastic, stopped using plastic containers, switched to glass and steel
  • After lifestyle changes, his testosterone went up to 1,200 ng/dL with NO testosterone replacement therapy

This is consistent with Dr. Swan's research: reducing plastic exposure can have measurable, significant health improvements.

Hidden Sources of Plastic Chemicals

The interview revealed surprising places where endocrine-disrupting chemicals hide:

PFAS in Clothing

  • Nonstick cookware (PTFE/PFAS coatings)
  • Rain jackets and waterproof clothing
  • Yoga pants and workout clothes
  • School uniforms and sports uniforms
  • Stain-resistant furniture and carpets

Phthalates in Unexpected Places

  • Pesticides: Phthalates are added to pesticides to increase absorption into plants — the same reason they're in hand cream (to help it absorb into skin)
  • Coffee makers: Most drip coffee machines have plastic that contacts hot water
  • Paper cups: Lined with bisphenols (BPA/BPS)
  • Car air fresheners: Mercedes labels their fragrance cartridges as "Miscellaneous Dangerous Goods"
  • Scented candles and incense: Phthalates make scents last longer — linked to respiratory issues, cardiovascular disease

What Dr. Swan Does in Her Own Home

Dr. Swan's Personal Choices

  • Water: Uses a countertop distiller — removes ALL chemicals including fluoride, chlorine, and microplastics
  • Coffee: No plastic coffee machine — uses glass/stainless steel methods
  • Cookware: No nonstick — stainless steel and cast iron only
  • Storage: Glass and stainless steel, never plastic
  • Testing: Offers urine test kits to measure phthalate, bisphenol, and paraben levels

Joe Rogan's Changes

After his first interview with Dr. Swan, Joe Rogan made several changes:

  • Switched from a plastic drip coffee machine to a stainless steel French press with a steel water boiler
  • Stopped drinking from plastic or paper cups
  • Notes that French press coffee "tastes way better" — a bonus beyond the health benefits
  • Underwent plasma pheresis (blood filtering) to remove accumulated chemicals — reported feeling "lighter" with less inflammation the next day

Take Action Today

You don't need to overhaul your entire life at once. Start with the highest-impact changes:

  1. Replace your water bottle — switch from plastic to stainless steel (our top picks here)
  2. Replace your coffee setup — ditch the plastic coffee maker for a French press or pour-over
  3. Switch food storage — glass containers for microwave/freezer, steel for packed lunches
  4. Filter your water — reverse osmosis or distillation removes microplastics
