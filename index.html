Why Go Plastic-Free?

Microplastics — tiny fragments shed from plastic containers, bottles, and packaging — have been found in human blood, lungs, placenta, and breast milk. Studies suggest the average person ingests about 5 grams of microplastic per week, roughly the weight of a credit card.

The Science Is Clear

  • Heat accelerates shedding — Microwaving plastic containers releases billions of nanoplastics into food
  • Wear and tear matters — Scratched plastic containers leach more particles than new ones
  • BPA-free isn't enough — BPA replacements (BPS, BPF) show similar health concerns in studies
  • Long-term accumulation — Microplastics don't break down; they accumulate in your body over decades

The Good News

Switching is easier and cheaper than ever. Glass, stainless steel, and silicone alternatives perform better, last longer, and cost about the same as quality plastic. Here's our curated guide to the best plastic-free products in every category.
